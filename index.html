About Walmart

Walmart Inc. is an American multinational retail corporation operating a chain of hypermarkets, discount department stores, and grocery stores with over 4,700 locations in the US.

Founded in 1962, Walmart has grown to become one of the world's largest employers with approximately 2.1 million associates. The company is headquartered in Bentonville, Arkansas and operates 4,742 retail locations across the United States.

Walmart offers a wide range of employment opportunities, from entry-level positions starting at age 16 to specialized roles in pharmacy, management, and logistics. Associates are paid on a weekly basis, with average hourly wages of $15.50.

Why Work at Walmart?

Employees cite several reasons for choosing Walmart as an employer, including flexible scheduling options, opportunities for advancement, employee discounts, and comprehensive benefits packages. The company has invested heavily in associate development programs and educational benefits.

Company Culture

Based on 128,453 employee reviews, Walmart maintains a rating of 3.4 out of 5 stars. Associates frequently mention the fast-paced work environment, team-oriented culture, and various shift options as key aspects of the workplace experience.
