Retail Store Hiring (Cashier, Stocker, Cart Attendant)

Store-level hiring typically takes 1-2 weeks from application to start date.

1

Online Application (15-20 min)

Apply through careers site. Enter availability - flexibility with weekends helps.

2

Assessment Test (20-30 min)

Online test with work scenarios, customer service questions, basic math.

3

In-Person Interview (15-30 min)

One interview with hiring manager. Questions about availability, customer service, teamwork.

4

Background Check (3-5 days)

Standard criminal background check after conditional offer.

5

Orientation & Training

1-2 days orientation covering policies, then on-the-job training.

Warehouse/Distribution Center Hiring

Warehouse hiring is the fastest - often same-week from application to start.

1

Online Application (15-20 min)

Same online process. Specify shift preference (day, evening, overnight).

2

Assessment Test (20-30 min)

Similar assessment. May include physical requirements acknowledgment.

3

Group Interview or Hiring Event

Often same-day hiring events. Group interviews are common. Very efficient process.

4

Drug Test (Required)

Pre-employment drug test required for all warehouse positions.

5

Background Check + Start

Background check often runs while you start training. Can begin within days.

Corporate/Salaried Hiring (Accountant, Analyst, Manager)

Corporate hiring is more rigorous with multiple interview rounds over 3-6 weeks.

1

Application + Resume (30-45 min)

Apply online with resume and cover letter. Complete job-specific questions.

2

Recruiter Screen (30 min phone)

Initial call with HR/recruiter to discuss role, salary expectations, background.

3

Technical/Skills Assessment

Role-dependent: Excel test for analysts, coding for tech, case studies for strategy.

4

Panel Interviews (2-4 rounds)

Interviews with hiring manager, team members, and senior leadership. May include case presentations.

5

Background Check + Offer

Thorough background check including education verification. Offer includes relocation if applicable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get hired at Walmart?

Hiring time varies: Retail positions (1-2 weeks), Warehouse jobs (1 week, often faster), Corporate roles (3-6 weeks with multiple interviews).

Is the hiring process different for warehouse vs store jobs?

Yes. Warehouse hiring is typically faster (often same-week) with group interviews. Store positions take 1-2 weeks with individual interviews. Corporate roles require multiple rounds over several weeks.

Does Walmart hire on the spot?

Warehouse and distribution center positions often offer same-day or next-day hiring during job fairs. Retail store positions rarely hire on the spot. Corporate roles never hire immediately.

What assessment do I need to take?

Hourly positions require an online assessment (20-30 min) covering work scenarios and personality. Corporate roles may have technical assessments, case studies, or skills tests specific to the role.
