Walmart Hiring Process: Complete Guide for 2026

How to Get Hired at Walmart

Getting hired at Walmart is a straightforward process that typically takes 1-2 weeks. The company hires for thousands of positions across its 4,742 locations, making it one of the most accessible employers for job seekers at all experience levels.

Most positions at Walmart require no prior experience, and the company provides comprehensive training for new associates. The minimum age to work at Walmart is 16 for most positions.

Hiring Steps

1

Online Application

Complete the online application at the careers portal. Takes 15-20 minutes. Include availability and work history.

2

Assessment Test

Complete the online assessment covering work scenarios, personality, and basic math. Takes 20-30 minutes.

3

Interview

Phone screening or in-person interview with a hiring manager. Typically 15-30 minutes. Be prepared to discuss availability.

4

Background Check

Standard background check process. Results typically come back within 3-5 business days.

5

Job Offer & Orientation

Receive offer, complete paperwork, and attend new hire orientation. Orientation covers company policies and job training.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get hired at Walmart?

The Walmart hiring process typically takes 1-2 weeks from application to job offer. Some positions may be filled faster, especially during peak hiring periods.

What are the steps to get hired at Walmart?

The Walmart hiring process includes: 1) Online application, 2) Assessment test, 3) Phone or in-person interview, 4) Background check, 5) Job offer and orientation.

Is it hard to get hired at Walmart?

Walmart hires thousands of associates and is generally considered accessible for entry-level job seekers. The key is completing the application and assessment thoroughly.

Does Walmart hire on the spot?

While rare, Walmart occasionally offers same-day hiring during job fairs and urgent hiring events. Most candidates go through the standard 1-2 week process.
