Walmart Age Requirements: Minimum Age to Work in 2026

Minimum Age Overview

The minimum age to work at Walmart is 16 years old for most entry-level positions. However, certain positions and departments require employees to be at least 18 years of age.

Minors (employees under 18) must comply with federal and state labor laws regarding work hours, break times, and prohibited tasks. A work permit may be required depending on your state.

Work Hour Restrictions for Minors

Federal law and most states restrict work hours for employees under 18:

  • School days: Maximum 3-4 hours
  • Non-school days: Maximum 8 hours
  • Weekly limit during school: 18 hours
  • No work before 7am or after certain evening hours
  • Specific rules vary by state

Frequently Asked Questions

How old do you have to be to work at Walmart?

The minimum age to work at Walmart is 16 years old for most positions. Some roles require employees to be at least 18.

Can a 16 year old work at Walmart?

Yes, Walmart hires 16-year-olds for entry-level positions such as cashier, cart attendant, and sales floor associate with work permit requirements.

What jobs can a 17 year old do at Walmart?

17-year-olds can work as cashiers, stockers, cart attendants, and sales associates. Restrictions apply to operating certain equipment.

What positions require you to be 18 at Walmart?

Positions requiring age 18+ include: operating heavy machinery, working in the deli/bakery, pharmacy technician, overnight shifts, and management roles.
