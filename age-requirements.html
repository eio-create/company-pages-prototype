Jobs for 16-17 Year Olds

Walmart offers several positions for minors in retail stores:

Available Positions

  • Cashier / Front-End Associate
  • Cart Attendant / Lot Associate
  • Sales Floor Associate
  • Fitting Room Attendant
  • Greeter (some locations)

Requirements

  • Work permit (required in most states)
  • Parent/guardian consent
  • School schedule accommodation
  • Limited to daytime hours

Warehouse: 18+ Required

All warehouse and distribution center positions require employees to be 18 or older. This is due to:

  • OSHA regulations for operating powered equipment
  • Physical demands and lifting requirements
  • Overnight shift availability
  • Safety-sensitive nature of the work

Prohibited Tasks for Minors

Even in allowed positions, employees under 18 cannot perform certain tasks:

  • Operating forklifts or pallet jacks
  • Using balers or compactors
  • Working with meat slicers
  • Operating ovens (bakery/deli)
  • Climbing ladders over 6 feet
  • Working on loading docks
  • Overnight shifts
  • Working alone in the store

Frequently Asked Questions

How old do you have to be to work at Walmart?

The minimum age is 16 for retail store positions (cashier, cart attendant). Warehouse positions require age 18+. Corporate internships may accept 18+, full-time corporate roles typically require 21+.

Can a 16 year old work at Walmart?

Yes, 16-year-olds can work as cashiers, cart attendants, and sales floor associates in retail stores. Work permits required. Cannot work overnight, operate equipment, or work in warehouse/distribution.

Can a 17 year old work in the warehouse?

No, all warehouse and distribution center positions require employees to be 18 or older due to equipment operation and safety requirements.

What jobs require 18+ at Walmart?

Positions requiring 18+: All warehouse jobs, pharmacy technician, deli/bakery, overnight shifts, forklift operation, auto technician, management, and corporate roles.
