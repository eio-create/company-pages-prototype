Walmart Interview Questions: What to Expect in 2026

Common Interview Questions

Walmart interviews focus on customer service, teamwork, and availability. Here are the most common questions:

"Why do you want to work at Walmart?"

Mention specific aspects like flexible scheduling, growth opportunities, or the company culture. Show you've researched the company.

"Tell me about a time you provided great customer service."

Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Even non-work examples are acceptable.

"What is your availability?"

Be honest but flexible. Weekend and holiday availability is often important for retail positions.

"How would you handle a difficult customer?"

Emphasize staying calm, listening to the customer, and finding solutions while following company policies.

"Describe a time you worked as part of a team."

Share an example showing collaboration, communication, and how you contributed to team success.

Interview Tips

  • Arrive 10-15 minutes early
  • Bring a copy of your resume
  • Dress in business casual attire
  • Research Walmart beforehand
  • Prepare questions to ask the interviewer
  • Be ready to discuss your availability

Frequently Asked Questions

What questions does Walmart ask in interviews?

Walmart interviews typically include questions about availability, customer service scenarios, teamwork experience, and why you want to work there.

How long is a Walmart interview?

Walmart interviews typically last 15-30 minutes. Group interviews for seasonal hiring may be shorter.

What should I wear to a Walmart interview?

Business casual is recommended for Walmart interviews. Clean, neat appearance is important. Avoid overly casual clothing like ripped jeans.

How do I prepare for a Walmart interview?

Research Walmart, prepare examples of customer service and teamwork, know your availability, and practice common interview questions.
