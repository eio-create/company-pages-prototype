Pay Overview

Walmart compensation varies significantly by position type. Hourly retail workers earn $12.00-$18/hr, warehouse workers earn $17-22/hr (plus shift differentials), and corporate employees receive annual salaries ranging from $50,000 to $150,000+.

All Walmart employees are paid on a weekly schedule. Hourly workers can access earned wages early through the Even app, while salaried employees receive standard direct deposit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average pay at Walmart?

Pay varies by position type: Hourly retail positions average $14-18/hr, warehouse workers earn $17-22/hr, and corporate salaries range $50,000-$120,000+/year. Overall hourly average is $15.50/hr.

How often does Walmart pay employees?

Walmart pays all employees on a weekly basis. Hourly workers can access earned wages early through the Even app. Salaried employees receive standard payroll deposits.

Do warehouse workers make more than store employees?

Yes, warehouse positions typically pay $2-4/hr more than equivalent retail store positions due to physical demands and fixed schedules. Warehouse workers can also earn shift differentials of $1-2/hr for nights.

What is the highest paying hourly job at Walmart?

Among hourly positions, Pharmacy Technician ($18-22/hr) and Forklift Operator ($18-21/hr) are the highest paying. Skilled warehouse roles and pharmacy positions consistently earn above average.

What do corporate employees make at Walmart?

Corporate salaries vary widely by role: Entry-level analysts earn $50,000-70,000, mid-level managers $80,000-120,000, and directors/VPs $150,000+. Corporate employees also receive annual bonuses of 10-20% of salary.
